  • Foldable Phones are Back, and They Aren’t your Dad’s Old Flip Phone

Since the start of the cell phone, innovative designers thought up the best ways to bring the most convenience to the consumer. The first innovation came in the form of reducing the overall size and shape of the phone from a brick to a wallet. To make the phone more compact, hinges were added that allowed the screen to lay flat on the keypad when not in use known primarily as the “flip phone” design. The next big leap forward was the touch screen. Buttons became nearly obsolete as the entire display was programmable to the consumers every need. From keyboard to unique applications, the touch screen display was more versatile in every way.

These changes have brought us to the forefront of the next hurtle, how to conveniently package the largest display possible under the constraint of a portable size. Foldable phones sought out to achieve this goal with their flexible display.
